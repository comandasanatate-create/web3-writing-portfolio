# Step-by-Step Guide: How to Bridge to Arbitrum in 2026

## Introduction

As Ethereum gas fees remain relatively high, many users are moving to Layer 2 networks like Arbitrum to enjoy faster and cheaper transactions. Bridging assets from Ethereum mainnet to Arbitrum is one of the first essential steps for anyone entering the Layer 2 ecosystem.

This guide walks you through the safest and simplest way to bridge to Arbitrum in 2026.

---

## What Is Arbitrum?

Arbitrum is a Layer 2 scaling solution for Ethereum that uses optimistic rollups to reduce transaction costs and increase throughput while maintaining Ethereum-level security.

Key benefits include:

* Lower gas fees
* Faster confirmations
* Full EVM compatibility
* Large DeFi ecosystem

Because of these advantages, Arbitrum has become one of the most widely used Layer 2 networks.

---

## What You Need Before Bridging

Before you start, make sure you have:

* A MetaMask wallet
* Some ETH on Ethereum mainnet
* A small extra amount of ETH for gas fees
* A secure internet connection

⚠️ Always verify URLs before connecting your wallet.

---

## Step 1: Open the Official Arbitrum Bridge

1. Go to the official Arbitrum Bridge
2. Double-check the URL to avoid phishing
3. Click **Connect Wallet**
4. Select **MetaMask**

After connecting, you will see the bridge interface.

---

## Step 2: Select Networks

The bridge usually auto-selects:

* From: Ethereum
* To: Arbitrum One

If not:

1. Set **From = Ethereum Mainnet**
2. Set **To = Arbitrum One**

---

## Step 3: Enter Amount to Bridge

1. Enter the amount of ETH you want to bridge
2. Leave some ETH on mainnet for gas
3. Review estimated fees

💡 First time? Start with a small test amount.

---

## Step 4: Approve and Confirm

You will complete two actions:

### 4.1 Approve (sometimes skipped for ETH)

* MetaMask pops up
* Review gas fee
* Click **Confirm**

### 4.2 Bridge transaction

* MetaMask opens again
* Review details
* Click **Confirm**

---

## Step 5: Wait for Completion

Typical bridging time:

* Deposit to Arbitrum: ~10–15 minutes

You can track the transaction in the bridge UI.

Once complete:

* Switch MetaMask network to **Arbitrum One**
* You should see your bridged ETH

---

## Common Beginner Mistakes

Avoid these:

❌ Using unofficial bridge links
❌ Bridging entire wallet balance
❌ Forgetting gas on mainnet
❌ Panicking during normal waiting time
❌ Sending funds directly to Arbitrum without bridging

---

## Security Best Practices

Stay safe by:

* Bookmarking official bridge
* Using hardware wallet for large amounts
* Testing with small transactions first
* Avoiding links from DMs
* Checking transaction details carefully

---

## Conclusion

Bridging to Arbitrum is a simple but critical step for accessing cheaper and faster Ethereum transactions in 2026. By following the steps above and maintaining strong security habits, users can safely move funds and begin exploring the Arbitrum ecosystem.

Start small, verify everything, and enjoy the benefits of Layer 2.
